Paracetamol

Medicine Details

  • Drug Interactions: Metoclopramide, Chloramphenicol, Narcotics, Anticonvulsants, Acetaminophen, Ranitidine, Ketoconazole, Busulfan.
  • Drug-Food Interactions: Alcohol, caffeine, jellies (pectin). Take with food. Do not take on an empty stomach.
  • Adverse Effects: Swelling, increased heartbeat, nausea, loss of appetite, abdominal pain, seizures.
  • Contraindications: Hepatic & kidney failure, hypersensitivity. Safe for: Pregnant and lactating women.
  • Dose:
    • Age 10-16 yrs: 500mg tablet every 6 hours. Not more than 4g or 8 tablets in 24 hours.
    • Adults: 650mg every 4-6 hours.
    • Age 6-9 yrs: 250mg tablet every 6 hours.
    • Infants: Dose must be determined by a doctor based on body weight.
  • Missed Dose: Give the next dose when needed. Do not give a double dose.
  • Precaution: Check with a doctor immediately if any adverse effects are observed.
