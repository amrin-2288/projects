Aspirin

Medicine Details

  • Drug Interactions: Warfarin, heparin, NSAIDs, corticosteroids, antacids.
  • Drug-Food Interactions: Alcohol (increases risk of bleeding). Avoid excessive caffeine.
  • Adverse Effects: Nausea, stomach pain, bleeding, tinnitus, rash, dizziness, allergic reactions.
  • Contraindications: Hypersensitivity, peptic ulcer disease, bleeding disorders, asthma, pregnancy.
  • Precautions: Avoid in children under 12 years with viral infections (risk of Reye's syndrome).
  • Missed Dose: Take as soon as you remember. Skip if close to the next dose. Do not double dose.
  • Dosage:
    • For pain/fever: Adults - 325-650 mg every 4-6 hours.
    • For heart attack prevention: 81-325 mg daily (as prescribed).
